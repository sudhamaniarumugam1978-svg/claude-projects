# ADR-0007 — AI Governance Framework

- **Status:** Accepted
- **Date:** Phase 0
- **Deciders:** Product, AI, Architecture, Security & Privacy

## Context

StockSense makes money-affecting recommendations from AI over data the retailer does not
fully control. Prior ADRs each governed one facet of this: [ADR-0003](0003-human-in-the-loop-decisioning.md)
keeps humans as the terminal decider, [ADR-0004](0004-explainable-ai-mandate.md) mandates
explainability, and [ADR-0006](0006-layer-over-systems-of-record.md) accepts dependence on
external data. What is missing is a **single, unifying governance framework** that states
*how* AI decisions are controlled end to end — so that hallucination prevention, confidence
handling, fallback, override, auditing, versioning, traceability, data quality, fairness,
and privacy are not scattered, implicit, or left to individual engineers' judgment.

Without such a framework, three failure modes are likely:

1. **Silent trust erosion** — an ungoverned model emits a confident-sounding but unfounded
   recommendation, a retailer acts on it, loses money, and never trusts the product again.
2. **Un-auditable decisions** — when a recommendation is later questioned, no one can
   reconstruct which model, prompt, data, and logic produced it.
3. **Inconsistent safeguards** — each agent handles low confidence, missing data, or bias
   differently, so the product's promises hold in some paths and quietly fail in others.

Governance is therefore not a compliance afterthought; it is the mechanism that makes the
[AI Philosophy](../../product/12-ai-philosophy.md) and [Product Principles](../../product/10-product-principles.md)
*enforceable* rather than aspirational.

## Decision

Adopt a platform-wide **AI Governance Framework** that every agent and AI-produced output
must comply with. The framework is normative: an output that violates it is **not emitted**.
It is composed of the fourteen controls below.

### 1. Purpose and scope

- **Purpose.** Ensure that every AI-influenced decision in StockSense is *grounded,
  explained, confidence-scored, traceable, fair, privacy-preserving, and reversible by a
  human.*
- **In scope.** All AI/ML-produced artifacts: demand forecasts, risk findings, business
  impact estimates, recommendations, executive summaries, and any assistant-interface
  responses — across all agents defined in the [Agent Catalog](../../agents/agent-catalog.md).
- **Out of scope.** Deterministic, non-inferential computation (e.g., arithmetic KPI
  rollups) is subject to auditing and traceability but not to hallucination/confidence
  controls. Autonomous *execution* remains governed by [ADR-0003](0003-human-in-the-loop-decisioning.md).
- **Applies to.** Every layer — Ingestion, Foundation, Analysis, Decision, Delivery — and
  the cross-cutting Orchestrator and Learning/Feedback agents.

### 2. Hallucination prevention strategy

StockSense treats a fabricated rationale or unsupported number as a **defect of the same
severity as a security bug**. Prevention is layered ("defense in depth"):

- **Grounding requirement.** Every claim in an output must resolve to `evidence_refs`
  pointing at real, validated data (see control 10). Claims without evidence are stripped or
  the output is withheld.
- **Retrieval over recall.** Assistant-interface and narrative generation are grounded in
  the retailer's own data model, never in the model's parametric world knowledge.
- **Numeric integrity.** Quantities (reorder amounts, impact figures, forecasts) are
  produced by the deterministic Analysis/Forecast pipeline, **not** free-generated by a
  language model. Any AI narrative must cite the pipeline value, and a validation step
  rejects narratives whose numbers disagree with their `evidence_refs`.
- **Constrained outputs.** Recommendation and summary generation use schema-constrained
  outputs so the model fills defined fields rather than inventing structure.
- **Contradiction checks.** The Recommendation Agent cross-checks a candidate output
  against its supporting data; internal contradictions cause rejection or downgrade to a
  clearly-labeled informational note.
- **"Evidence or silence."** Consistent with the [AI Philosophy](../../product/12-ai-philosophy.md),
  when grounding cannot be established the system withholds rather than guesses.

### 3. Confidence score methodology

- Every AI artifact carries a **calibrated `confidence` value in `[0,1]`** on the shared
  message envelope ([Agent Architecture §5.2](../13-agent-architecture.md#52-canonical-message-envelope-conceptual)).
- **Composition.** A recommendation's confidence is a principled combination of its upstream
  contributors — forecast confidence, risk-finding likelihood, and the `data_quality_score`
  of the underlying data. Confidence can only be **weakened** by a poor data-quality score,
  never inflated by it.
- **Calibration, not vibes.** Confidence must be *calibrated*: outputs asserted at 0.8 must
  succeed about 80% of the time. Calibration is measured against realized outcomes by the
  Learning/Feedback Agent and reported as a first-class quality metric
  ([Success Metrics](../../business/11-success-metrics.md)).
- **Miscalibration is a defect.** A persistent gap between stated and realized confidence
  is triaged like any other bug, not tolerated as noise.
- **Sparse-data honesty.** With thin history, the Forecast Agent falls back to simpler
  methods and *lowers confidence honestly* rather than projecting false certainty.

### 4. Confidence thresholds (Low, Medium, High)

Platform-wide bands standardize how confidence changes system behavior. Exact numeric
boundaries are tuned per outcome during Phase 1 and monitored for calibration; the **bands
and their semantics are fixed here**.

| Band | Indicative range | Meaning | Default system behavior |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| **High** | ≥ 0.80 | Well-supported, calibrated | Surface as a primary, actionable recommendation; eligible for opt-in assisted execution (V3, per [ADR-0003](0003-human-in-the-loop-decisioning.md)) |
| **Medium** | 0.50 – 0.79 | Plausible but uncertain | Surface as a recommendation **explicitly flagged as uncertain**; never auto-executed; invites human judgment |
| **Low** | < 0.50 | Weak / insufficient support | **Not emitted as a recommendation.** May appear only as a clearly-labeled informational signal or a request for more data (see control 5) |

Thresholds are **evidence gates**, not cosmetics: the band an output lands in
deterministically selects its downstream treatment.

### 5. Fallback behavior when confidence is insufficient

When confidence falls in the **Low** band, or a mandatory element (per control 11) cannot
be produced, the system degrades gracefully in this priority order:

1. **Downgrade, don't fabricate.** Convert the would-be recommendation into a labeled
   *informational observation* ("we see a possible pattern but cannot yet recommend an
   action confidently").
2. **Fall back to a simpler, defensible method.** Prefer a transparent baseline (e.g., a
   conservative reorder heuristic) over a fragile complex model, and say so.
3. **Request more/better data.** Where a specific data gap is the cause, surface a targeted
   request rather than a guess.
4. **Withhold and log.** If none of the above yields a trustworthy output, emit nothing to
   the user and record the suppression in the audit log with its reason.

Under no circumstances does insufficient confidence result in a confidently-worded
recommendation. Silence is an acceptable output; false certainty is not.

### 6. Human override workflow

Consistent with [ADR-0003](0003-human-in-the-loop-decisioning.md), the human is the
terminal decision-maker. The governed override workflow is:

1. **Present.** Every recommendation is shown with its reasoning, confidence (and band),
   supporting data, and business impact.
2. **Decide.** The user may **Approve**, **Modify** (adjust quantity/timing/action), or
   **Reject**, with an optional reason.
3. **Record.** The decision, any modification, the reason, the actor identity, and a
   timestamp are captured immutably (control 7) and tied to the recommendation's `trace_id`.
4. **Learn — never overrule.** Overrides feed the Learning/Feedback Agent to improve future
   forecasts, recommendations, and calibration. The system **tunes** from human decisions;
   it never reverses or re-issues an action the human rejected.
5. **Escalation.** Repeated overrides of a given recommendation type raise a governance
   signal for review of the underlying model/prompt.

### 7. Audit logging requirements

Every AI-influenced decision must be reconstructable after the fact. For each emitted
artifact the system records an **immutable, append-only** audit record containing:

- `trace_id` linking the full decision chain (owned by the Orchestrator).
- Input `evidence_refs` (which data, as-of timestamps) and its `data_quality_score`.
- The producing agent(s), the **model version** (control 9) and **prompt version**
  (control 8) used.
- The output payload, its `confidence` and band, and the reasoning/impact fields.
- Any fallback or suppression event and its reason (control 5).
- The human decision (approve/modify/reject), actor, reason, and timestamp (control 6).

Audit records are **tamper-evident**, access-controlled, and retained per the data-retention
policy (control 14). Deterministic KPI computations are logged for traceability even though
they are exempt from confidence controls.

### 8. Prompt versioning strategy

- **Prompts are versioned artifacts**, treated as code: source-controlled, reviewed, and
  released, never edited live in production.
- Each prompt has a **stable identifier and semantic version**; every AI output records the
  exact prompt version used (control 7).
- **Changes are gated.** A prompt change requires review and evaluation against a regression
  set before release; material changes are rolled out progressively and are reversible.
- **Reproducibility.** Given an audit record, the exact prompt text that produced an output
  can be retrieved.

### 9. Model version tracking

- Every model (forecasting model, any LLM, embeddings, ranking logic) carries an
  **immutable version/identifier**, recorded on every artifact it influences.
- **Pinned in production.** Models are pinned; upgrades are deliberate, evaluated against
  benchmarks and calibration, staged, and reversible (rollback path required).
- **Change provenance.** The audit log ties each output to its model version, enabling
  "which model version produced this?" and post-hoc analysis of a model change's impact.
- **Drift monitoring.** The Learning/Feedback Agent watches for accuracy/calibration drift
  after any model change and can trigger rollback.

### 10. Recommendation traceability

- The **Orchestrator** assigns and propagates a `trace_id` so any recommendation can be
  followed end-to-end: raw source → Integration → Data Quality → Foundation → Forecast/Risk/
  Analytics → Recommendation → Delivery → human decision.
- Every message carries `evidence_refs` pointing at the specific upstream inputs used, so
  traceability is *structural*, not reconstructed after the fact — this operationalizes the
  "no black boxes" principle from [ADR-0001](0001-decision-intelligence-not-dashboards.md).
- Any single output can produce a **complete provenance trail** on demand.

### 11. Explainability enforcement

- This framework **subsumes and enforces** [ADR-0004](0004-explainable-ai-mandate.md): every
  recommendation and executive summary must carry **reasoning, confidence, supporting data,
  and business impact**, or it is not emitted.
- Enforcement is a **validation at the Recommendation/Executive Agent boundary**, made
  possible because the fields accrue as messages flow through the pipeline.
- **Explainability constrains model choice**: an explainable method is preferred over a more
  accurate but opaque one. "Explanation completeness" remains a **100% hard-requirement KPI**
  ([Success Metrics](../../business/11-success-metrics.md)).

### 12. Handling of missing or low-quality data

- The **Data Quality Agent** validates, de-duplicates, and reconciles ingested data and
  attaches a `data_quality_score` that **travels with the data** downstream
  ([Agent Catalog](../../agents/agent-catalog.md)).
- **Quality is visible, never hidden.** Poor data lowers confidence (control 3) and can push
  an output into the Low band (controls 4–5); it is never silently cleaned and presented as
  trustworthy.
- **No silent interpolation.** Gaps in the inventory model are surfaced, not invented.
  Discrepancy signals (e.g., shrink) are exposed rather than absorbed.
- Executive summaries explicitly flag coverage gaps (e.g., a store with stale data).

### 13. Bias and fairness considerations

- **Systematic-error monitoring.** Forecasts and recommendations are monitored for
  consistent bias by segment (e.g., persistently under-forecasting a category, store, or
  supplier). Detected bias is surfaced, not hidden.
- **Reliability transparency.** Where a model is unreliable for a segment, that limitation is
  communicated (lower confidence, explicit caveat) rather than masked by a uniform score.
- **No protected-attribute targeting.** StockSense governs *inventory* decisions; models
  must not use or proxy sensitive personal attributes. The product does not profile
  individuals (consistent with content and privacy constraints).
- **Feedback-loop fairness.** The Learning/Feedback Agent is monitored so that override
  signals do not entrench bias (e.g., systematically de-prioritizing a category).

### 14. Privacy and data protection

- **Data minimization.** StockSense operates on inventory, sales, and supplier data; it does
  not require end-customer PII. Where source systems expose PII, the Integration Agent
  minimizes and avoids ingesting it unless strictly necessary.
- **Tenant isolation.** Each retailer's data is logically isolated; models and prompts must
  not leak one tenant's data into another's outputs.
- **Least privilege.** Access to external systems of record is read-oriented and
  least-privilege ([ADR-0006](0006-layer-over-systems-of-record.md)); access to audit logs is
  role-restricted.
- **Retention & deletion.** Audit and operational data follow a defined retention policy with
  honored deletion requests; retention windows are set to satisfy auditability without
  over-collection.
- **In transit & at rest.** Retailer data and audit records are protected with encryption
  and standard security controls (detailed in Phase 1 security design).

## Rationale

- **Trust is the adoption gate.** Our skeptical, non-expert users
  ([Personas](../../product/08-user-personas.md)) will only rely on advice that is grounded,
  explained, and reversible. Governance is what makes that reliable at scale.
- **One framework beats scattered safeguards.** Unifying these controls guarantees the
  product's promises hold on *every* path, not just the happy path.
- **Auditability is a business asset.** Being able to reconstruct any decision protects the
  retailer, protects StockSense, and accelerates debugging and continuous improvement.
- **It future-proofs compliance.** Regulatory expectations for AI transparency, fairness, and
  data protection are rising; building governance in now is far cheaper than retrofitting it.

## Trade-offs

- **Engineering overhead.** Carrying provenance, versions, confidence, and quality scores
  through every message and persisting immutable audit records adds real cost and latency.
- **Accuracy vs. governance.** We will decline more outputs (Low-band withholding) and prefer
  explainable methods, occasionally sacrificing marginal accuracy or "wow" for trust.
- **Velocity friction.** Prompt/model changes must pass review, evaluation, and staged
  rollout — slower than editing in place, deliberately.
- **Storage and retention cost.** Comprehensive audit logging consumes storage and imposes
  data-lifecycle obligations.
- **Conservative UX.** Honest uncertainty and withholding may feel less confident to users
  than competitors' unqualified assertions — an intentional trade in favor of durable trust.

## Alternatives Considered

1. **No unifying framework — govern per-agent.** Rejected: produces inconsistent safeguards
   and gaps; the product's promises would hold unevenly.
2. **Lightweight guidelines instead of enforced controls.** Rejected: best-effort governance
   erodes exactly like best-effort explainability ([ADR-0004](0004-explainable-ai-mandate.md));
   only enforced-and-validated controls are durable.
3. **Maximize model autonomy and accuracy, add governance later.** Rejected: retrofitting
   traceability, calibration, and audit is far costlier and less trustworthy than building
   them in; it also conflicts with [ADR-0003](0003-human-in-the-loop-decisioning.md).
4. **Log everything but do not gate outputs on confidence/quality.** Rejected: observability
   without enforcement still lets unfounded, low-confidence recommendations reach users.
5. **Defer privacy/fairness to a separate future initiative.** Rejected: they are inseparable
   from trustworthy AI governance and cheap to establish as principles now.

## Consequences

- **Governance is a first-class, cross-cutting concern** in the
  [Agent Architecture](../13-agent-architecture.md): the message envelope's `confidence`,
  `evidence_refs`, and `data_quality_score` fields, plus the Orchestrator's `trace_id`, are
  the load-bearing mechanisms for this ADR.
- **New governance metrics** join the [Success Metrics](../../business/11-success-metrics.md):
  confidence calibration error, Low-band suppression rate, override rate by type, audit
  completeness, and detected-bias incidents.
- **The Recommendation/Executive boundary becomes the enforcement point** for hallucination,
  confidence-band, and explainability checks.
- **Prompts and models become versioned, reviewed release artifacts** with reproducibility
  and rollback requirements, adding process the team must adopt from Phase 1.
- **A future governance roadmap** is established (below) and tracked.
- This ADR **complements and reinforces** ADRs [0001](0001-decision-intelligence-not-dashboards.md),
  [0003](0003-human-in-the-loop-decisioning.md), [0004](0004-explainable-ai-mandate.md),
  [0005](0005-multi-agent-orchestration.md), and [0006](0006-layer-over-systems-of-record.md);
  it does not supersede any of them.

## Future Governance Roadmap

Aligned with the [Product Roadmap](../../planning/14-product-roadmap.md):

- **V1 (foundation).** Confidence scoring and bands; Low-band withholding and fallbacks;
  explainability enforcement; core audit logging with `trace_id`; model/prompt version
  stamping; data-quality scoring; data minimization and tenant isolation.
- **V2 (operationalize).** Automated calibration monitoring and drift detection; systematic
  bias monitoring by segment; prompt/model regression-evaluation gates in the release
  pipeline; governance dashboards for internal review; override-driven learning with fairness
  safeguards.
- **V3 (assurance & scale).** Governance controls required to unlock opt-in assisted
  execution (demonstrated calibration + reliability gates); enterprise-grade audit exports and
  policy controls; formal fairness reviews; alignment with emerging external AI-governance and
  data-protection standards; independent review readiness.
