# 02 — Mission Statement

## Mission

> **To help every retailer prevent inventory problems before they happen — by turning
> their data into clear, explained, and trustworthy decisions.**

---

## The Mission, Unpacked

Each clause of the mission statement is a deliberate commitment, not decoration.

| Clause | Commitment |
| --- | --- |
| **"every retailer"** | We serve independent and mid-sized retailers, not only enterprises. Sophistication that today requires a data team must become accessible to a single-store owner. |
| **"prevent … before they happen"** | We are proactive, not reactive. Our value is measured in problems avoided, not problems reported. |
| **"inventory problems"** | Our focus is disciplined: stockouts, overstock/dead stock, demand shifts, and inventory risk. We are not an ERP, POS, or CRM. |
| **"turning their data"** | We work on top of the retailer's existing systems of record. We do not require them to replace what they have. |
| **"clear, explained"** | Every output carries its reasoning. We never emit an unexplained AI decision. |
| **"trustworthy decisions"** | Every recommendation carries a confidence score and supporting evidence, and the human always makes the final call. |

---

## What This Mission Rules Out

A good mission excludes as much as it includes. This mission explicitly rules out:

- Becoming a general-purpose business suite (ERP/POS/CRM ambitions).
- Competing on the number of dashboards or charts.
- Shipping AI outputs that cannot be explained or defended.
- Automating away the retailer's authority over their own business.

See [Product Scope](06-product-scope.md) for the full boundary definition and
[Product Principles](10-product-principles.md) for the operating philosophy that enforces
this mission.
